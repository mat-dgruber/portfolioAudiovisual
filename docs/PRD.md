PRD — Cinematic Portfolio Website

1. Overview
   A high-end, cinematic professional portfolio designed for an audiovisual specialist (Director of Photography & Editor). The site serves as a visual resume to showcase a showreel and a curated gallery of projects, designed to attract high-ticket clients and production houses. Success is defined by a high-impact first impression and a seamless conversion from "viewer" to "lead" via the contact form or the "Work with me" CTA.
2. Problem Statement
   Audiovisual professionals often struggle to present their work in a way that reflects the quality of their production. Generic portfolios fail to capture the "mood" and "cinematic feel" of the work. Without this site, the professional relies on third-party platforms (Vimeo/YouTube) which offer no brand control and fragmented user experiences, potentially lowering their perceived value.
3. Target Users

Creative Directors & Producers: Searching for a specific aesthetic or technical skill set for an upcoming project. They are often on high-resolution monitors, looking for technical precision.
Brand Managers: Looking for a reliable partner to execute a commercial vision. They value reliability, a clean portfolio, and a professional "About" section.
Aspiring Collaborators: Peers looking for networking and inspiration.

4. What This App IS

A high-performance, single-page-centric portfolio with a deep-dark cinematic theme.
A dynamic project gallery with category filtering and full-screen video modals.
A conversion-optimized lead generation tool with a persistent "Work with me" floating action button.
A technically detailed "About" section showcasing equipment and expertise.
An animated experience using Anime.js to mirror the fluid transitions of professional video editing.

5. What This App is NOT

Not a video hosting platform (uses embeds from Vimeo/YouTube).
Not a social network or a community forum.
Not a blog or a long-form article platform.
Not an e-commerce store for selling digital assets.

7. Success Metrics

Primary: Number of "Work with me" clicks and Contact Form submissions per month.
Secondary: Average time on page (indicating engagement with the gallery), Showreel completion rate.

8. Constraints & Assumptions

Technical: Must maintain 60fps animations despite high-quality video backgrounds.
Assets: Assumes the user provides high-quality video files and embeds.
Performance: Must load quickly on mobile despite the cinematic assets.
